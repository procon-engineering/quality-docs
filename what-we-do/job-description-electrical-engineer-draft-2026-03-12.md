## About the Role

Procon designs and installs electrical systems on offshore wind turbine foundations. The Electrical Engineer is the technical engine behind the LV electrical package — the set of 60–100 engineering documents that defines everything from lighting on the main access platform to the control cabinet that talks to the turbine. You produce precise, standard-compliant deliverables, coordinate with vendors and contractors, and support the project through from design to site commissioning.

This is a delivery-focused role. You will work within a defined project scope with a Lead Engineer setting the technical direction. The quality of your output directly affects what gets fabricated and installed offshore.

---

## Responsibilities

### 1. Electrical Design Deliverables
Own the production and revision of the core LV electrical documents:
- **Schematics** — single line diagrams (SLD), control cabinet (FECB) wiring schematics, overvoltage protection (OVP) panel schematics
- **Cable documentation** — LV power cable lists, earthing cable lists, cable gland lists
- **Equipment documentation** — equipment lists, electrical tagging schedules (RDS-PP), load lists
- **Studies** — illumination calculations, earthing and lightning protection studies, load flow and power studies
- **Installation documents** — installation check and test sheets, cable routing drawings, layout support

Every document you issue has your name on it. It is your responsibility that the content is technically correct, internally consistent, and compliant with the applicable standards before it reaches the Lead Engineer for cross-document review.

### 2. Standards Compliance
Apply and maintain compliance with relevant IEC standards throughout the design:
- IEC 61892 (Mobile and fixed offshore units — electrical installations)
- IEC 60204-1 (Safety of machinery — electrical equipment)
- IEC 60332 (Cables and optical fibre cables — tests on electric and optical fibre cables under fire conditions)
- IEC 62305 (Lightning protection) — assessment scope
- RDS-PP tagging convention for equipment and cable identification

Identify when a design choice requires a deviation from a standard and flag it to the Lead Engineer for TQ or client approval. Do not self-approve deviations.

### 3. Technical Queries and Interfaces
Procon has a dedicated procurement department that manages supplier and subcontractor relationships. The Electrical Engineer's role is to provide the technical content that procurement acts on — not to manage supplier relationships directly.

Your interface responsibilities:
- Prepare technical input for **Technical Queries (TQs)** to WTG manufacturers and clients on electrical matters within your discipline; route to Lead Engineer for approval before issue
- Review vendor documentation returned via procurement (e.g. manufacturer wiring schematics, data sheets, cable certificates) for technical compliance against the Procon design
- Mark up deviations clearly and return findings to the Lead Engineer — procurement handles the commercial follow-up

### 4. Installation and Commissioning Support
Support the field phase of the project:
- Produce, maintain, and close out installation check and test sheets
- Respond to site queries within your technical scope
- Support Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) for electrical panels
- Review and accept redline mark-ups from site; update as-built documents

### 5. Document Quality
Maintain document discipline on your deliverables:
- Correct use of the project document ID format (e.g. NSC-HYND-NSCA-FOU-ENG-xxxxx)
- Reference tables updated on every revision — verify each reference entry, not just the title block
- Revision history complete with dates, authors, checker, and approver
- DMS version consistent with document title block version

## Decision Authority

### You can decide independently:
- Cable sizing and type selection within the project specification
- Equipment selection within a defined specification (brand, IP rating, mounting type)
- Electrical tagging (RDS-PP function and component codes) for new equipment within established project conventions
- Illumination calculation method and software settings
- Cable gland sizing and type based on cable OD and panel entry specification
- Torque values and installation methods per applicable standards and manufacturer data sheets

### You decide, then inform the Lead Engineer:
- Changes to an existing document that affect another document (e.g. revising a cable entry in the cable list that also appears in the earthing list)
- Selection of a component that is new to the project (not previously specified or approved)
- Interpretation of a standard when the standard is ambiguous or silent on a specific case

### You must escalate before acting:
- Any design change that affects scope, cost, or programme
- A client or WTG manufacturer requirement that conflicts with the Procon design (must be resolved via TQ)
- Non-conformance with a specified standard that cannot be resolved by a direct alternative — this requires a formal deviation or TQ
- Anything that touches the HV cable interface, the IAC scope boundary, or the WTG tower scope boundary — the Lead Engineer defines these interfaces
- Equipment or cable failures identified during FAT or installation

## Typical Tasks

### In a design phase week:
- Revise the LV cable list to reflect a schematic change, update revision history, verify that all affected reference documents are noted
- Run an illumination calculation for the MAP and ATP platforms using DIALUX (or equivalent); produce the study document with results and regulatory reference
- Prepare an earthing cable list from the earthing SLD; verify cable cross-sections against fault current requirements
- Update the electrical tagging schedule with new equipment tags; confirm RDS-PP codes against the project convention
- Draft a TQ response to a WTG manufacturer question about signal cable specification; route to Lead Engineer for approval

### In a procurement and fabrication phase week:
- Review a FECB manufacturer's proposed wiring schematic against the Procon schematic; mark up deviations for discussion
- Review and accept (or reject) cable certificate of conformity from supplier
- Answer a site query about cable gland torque values — find the applicable data sheet and provide the answer in writing
- Check that the cable gland list matches the FECB manufacturer's panel entry schedule

### In an installation and commissioning phase week:
- Complete the LV power cable installation check sheet for a set of WTGs; record IR test results against acceptance criteria
- Review site redline mark-ups from the installation team; assess whether changes require a document revision or can be accepted as minor deviations
- Witness or support the FECB FAT at the manufacturer's workshop; document test results and any punch list items
- Update cable list to reflect a field-installed cable substitution (different OD, same specification); flag to Lead Engineer for approval

## What Good Looks Like

- Documents are right first time — the Lead Engineer's cross-check review finds few or no errors
- Reference tables are always complete and accurate; ALL→NSCA corrections are never missed
- TQs are answered clearly and concisely, with the standard reference cited
- Site installation issues are resolved quickly because the installation documents are unambiguous
- Revision history is clean — anyone can look at a document and understand what changed and why

## What We Are Looking For

**Experience:**
- Background in LV electrical engineering, preferably in offshore, marine, or industrial EPC projects
- Familiarity with IEC offshore electrical standards (61892 series)
- Experience producing electrical schematics and cable documentation — not just checking them
- Exposure to offshore installation or commissioning is a strong advantage

**Tools — required:**
- **EPLAN Electric P8** — schematic design is done in EPLAN; you must be able to produce and revise schematics, manage the symbol library, and produce terminal plans and cable schedules from the project database
- **ETAP** (or equivalent, e.g. SKM PowerTools) — used for load flow, short circuit, and protection coordination calculations; results feed directly into the power studies document
- **Microsoft Excel** — cable lists, equipment lists, load lists, and tagging schedules are large, structured spreadsheets; you must be comfortable with formulas, structured tables, and cross-sheet referencing
- **Microsoft Word** — design reports, studies, and technical notes are produced in Word; document formatting and revision control within Word is part of the job

**Tools — advantageous:**
- **Simple scripting or programming** (Python, VBA, or similar) — the ability to write a short script to process a cable list, check for duplicates, or extract data from a spreadsheet saves significant manual effort and reduces errors; you do not need to be a developer, but you should be comfortable with the concept and willing to learn
- **DIALUX or RELUX** — illumination calculation software for lux studies

**AI tools:**
Procon actively uses AI tools to improve engineering quality and throughput. You are expected to use AI tools as a standard part of your workflow — not as a shortcut, but as a force multiplier:
- Use AI to cross-check documents, identify inconsistencies across large document sets, and flag potential errors before formal review
- Use AI to assist with drafting technical notes, TQ responses, and study write-ups — always verify and own the output
- Use AI to process and analyse structured data (cable lists, equipment lists) for patterns and cross-document consistency
- Procon's AI tooling is configured for this work; you will be onboarded to the specific tools and workflows used on your projects

**Mindset:**
- Detail-oriented without losing sight of the project purpose
- Comfortable asking for clarification rather than guessing
- Understands that a wrong number in a cable list becomes wrong hardware on a foundation in the North Sea
- Treats AI as a skilled tool — directs it clearly, verifies its output, and takes full professional responsibility for the result
