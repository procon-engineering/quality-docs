# Cable Standards — Electrical Discipline

Rules for cable specification in offshore wind projects. Apply to all projects unless client requirements override.

## IEC 60331 vs IEC 60332

**Rule:** IEC 60331 (fire resistant) is usually a mistake in Procon documents. IEC 60332 (flame retardant) is the correct standard for offshore wind cable specifications unless:
- The client specifically demands fire resistance, OR
- A TQ (Technical Query) justifies the requirement

**Action:** When reviewing any document that references IEC 60331, flag it and check for client requirement or TQ justification. If neither exists, mark as finding.

**Why:** IEC 60331 requires cables to maintain circuit integrity during fire. This is necessary for onshore building fire escape routes but is overkill for offshore wind foundations where the fire response is evacuation, not continued operation.

*Source: ENG-00006 (LV Design Report, Project 10260) review, confirmed by Magnus 2026-03-10*

## General Cable Construction — Offshore Wind

Standard requirements for offshore wind foundation cables:
- Halogen-free, flame-retardant (IEC 60332), low smoke
- Oil, weather, and UV resistant
- Hanging cables: IEC 60228 Class 5 (flexible stranding)
- Construction per IEC 60092-353
- Emergency/escape lighting: IEC 60332 (NOT 60331 — see above)
- AtoN cables on external platforms: armoured with bare copper

*Source: ENG-00006 (LV Design Report, Project 10260)*

## Standards Cross-Check Rule

**Rule:** Discipline specialists should cross-check every standard referenced in a document's standards table against the actual design decisions in the document body. Verify that cited standards are correctly applied, not just listed.
